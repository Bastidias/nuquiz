# Pressure Injury Staging

**Topic:** 2 — Safety and Infection Prevention and Control &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Ordered &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 2.1, 5-cross-tag
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

NPIAP (National Pressure Injury Advisory Panel) staging system for pressure injuries, updated in the 2016 NPIAP staging revision that replaced "pressure ulcer" terminology with "pressure injury" and added the Deep Tissue Pressure Injury and Unstageable categories as alternative classifications alongside the four numeric stages. NCLEX-RN tests this primarily under sub-objective 2.1 (Accident / Error / Injury Prevention) with cross-coverage in Topic 5 (skin integrity / pressure injury prevention, placeholder Tag `5-cross-tag`). CMS designates Stage 3, Stage 4, and Unstageable hospital-acquired pressure injuries as Hospital-Acquired Conditions (HACs) for which Medicare does not provide additional payment.

**Layout convention:** rows are the four numeric NPIAP stages in order of increasing tissue depth (Stage 1 → Stage 4), followed by the two alternative classifications (Unstageable, then Deep tissue injury) per NPIAP listing convention; the alternative classifications are not strictly sequential after Stage 4 but are listed last because their actual depth is unknown (Unstageable) or evolving (DTI). Columns progress left → right from observable surface findings to deeper tissue involvement to prognostic/regulatory implications. Each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Terminology change.** The 2016 NPIAP revision replaced "pressure ulcer" with "pressure injury" and renumbered stages using Arabic numerals (Stage 1, 2, 3, 4) instead of Roman numerals. The CMS HAC regulation language still references "Stage III and IV Pressure Ulcers" because the federal regulation predates the NPIAP terminology update; clinically, the cells use NPIAP 2016 language.
- **Unstageable and DTI are not sequential after Stage 4.** They are alternative classifications used when the stage cannot be assigned by visual inspection alone. NPIAP lists them after the numeric stages in its staging documents; this Concept follows that listing convention. The Layout convention line above documents this explicitly.
- **Stable heel eschar.** NPIAP guidance: stable, dry, adherent eschar on an ischemic limb or heel serves as the body's natural cover and should not be debrided. The wound is documented as Unstageable but eschar removal is not pursued. Captured in the Visual finding cell for Unstageable.
- **CMS HAC framing.** Stage 3, Stage 4, Unstageable, and DTI hospital-acquired pressure injuries are Hospital-Acquired Conditions under the CMS HAC payment provision when not present on admission; Medicare does not pay the higher MS-DRG that would otherwise apply. Stage 1 and Stage 2 are not on the HAC list. The cells reflect HAC status only for the stages on the CMS list.
- **Reversibility framing.** "Reversible" / "heals by re-epithelialization" / "heals by secondary intention" are pedagogy-consensus framings drawn from wound-care literature indexed in StatPearls / PMC. The underlying tissue-layer involvement is sourced to NPIAP staging definitions; the healing-mechanism mapping is a direct derivation (full-thickness loss requires secondary intention by definition; partial-thickness loss can re-epithelialize by definition). Cited to `[s2]` for the mapping.
- **Documentation framing.** Documentation cell items are derived from AHRQ pressure-injury prevention toolkit guidance on wound assessment elements (location, size, depth, drainage, undermining/tunneling, wound-bed appearance) plus NPIAP-specific re-staging rules. Cited per-item to `[s1]` (NPIAP staging) or `[s3]` (AHRQ wound-assessment elements) or `[s4]` (CMS HAC documentation).
- **Cross-Concept links.** Sibling `never-events-clinical.md` (T2 §2.1) covers Stage 3+ HAPI as a never-event category. Future Topic 5 Concept on skin integrity / repositioning will reference this Concept for the prevention angle (placeholder Tag `5-cross-tag`).
- **Out of scope.** Mucosal pressure injuries (NPIAP excludes mucosal injuries from the standard staging system and documents them descriptively); medical-device-related pressure injuries (use standard stages but track device source separately); Braden Scale risk assessment (lives in `fall-risk-factors-and-mitigations.md` peer Concept on risk-screening tools, not here); turning/repositioning protocols (Topic 5 prevention angle).

### Tricky distractors

- **Stage 1 vs DTI.** Both involve intact skin. Stage 1 shows non-blanchable erythema (red, may pale on relief of pressure briefly); DTI shows deep red, maroon, or purple discoloration of intact skin and may feel boggy or firm. Wrong-answer pattern: classifying a maroon-discolored intact-skin area as Stage 1 — DTI's deeper coloration and palpable tissue change are the disambiguators.
- **Stage 2 vs DTI blood-filled blister.** Stage 2 may present as a serum-filled blister (clear/straw-colored); a blood-filled blister is DTI, not Stage 2. Wrong-answer pattern: staging a hemorrhagic blister as Stage 2.
- **Stage 3 vs Stage 4.** Subcutaneous fat visible without exposed bone/muscle/tendon = Stage 3. Any palpable or visible fascia, muscle, tendon, ligament, cartilage, or bone = Stage 4. Wrong-answer pattern: staging a deep wound with visible fat as Stage 4 because it "looks deep."
- **Unstageable vs Stage 4.** If slough or eschar obscures the wound base, the wound is Unstageable until the base is visible — even if it is clinically obviously full-thickness. Wrong-answer pattern: assigning Stage 4 to a wound covered in eschar.
- **Stable heel eschar.** Do not debride stable, dry, adherent eschar on a heel or ischemic limb. Wrong-answer pattern: planning sharp debridement as the priority intervention for a stable heel eschar.
- **DTI evolution.** A DTI may resolve without tissue loss OR evolve rapidly to a Stage 3 / Stage 4 / Unstageable wound. Wrong-answer pattern: assuming DTI always becomes a deep wound or always resolves.
