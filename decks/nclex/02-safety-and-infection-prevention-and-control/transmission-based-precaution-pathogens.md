# Transmission-Based Precaution Pathogens

**Topic:** 2 — Safety and Infection Prevention and Control &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 2.9, IP-CJ
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

CDC Appendix A "Type and Duration of Precautions Recommended for Selected Infections and Conditions" (HICPAC 2007 with September 2024 updates) is the canonical primary source for assigning a specific pathogen to a specific Transmission-Based Precaution category and for stating the duration of precautions. The Concept inverts the sibling `isolation-precaution-types.md`: that Concept's Rows (Standard / Contact / Droplet / Airborne / Protective / Reverse / Contact + Droplet / Contact + Airborne) are the Values in this Concept's `precaution type` Column, so the engine can detect that — for example — TB and measles share `Airborne` as their precaution type, while VRE, MRSA, C. difficile, and norovirus share `Contact`. Rows are 10 of the most heavily NCLEX-tested pathogens. Columns are the four operational facts a nurse must recall at the bedside: precaution type, room type, additional measures, and duration of precautions.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Pathogen abbreviations are kept as canonical Row labels.** TB (Mycobacterium tuberculosis), MRSA (methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus), VRE (vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus), and C. difficile (Clostridioides difficile) are the forms used in NCSBN test stems and bedside vocabulary. Full taxonomic names live here in Notes rather than in Row labels.
- **Why the precaution-type Column reuses sibling Row labels exactly.** The sibling `isolation-precaution-types.md` defines the seven canonical precaution categories as Rows. Reusing those exact strings (`Standard`, `Contact`, `Droplet`, `Airborne`, `Protective / Reverse`, `Contact + Droplet`, `Contact + Airborne`) as Values in this Concept's `precaution type` Column lets the engine detect cross-Concept shared Values: e.g., the `Airborne` Value appears here on TB and measles and over there as a Row whose attributes (room, PPE, duration framework) are fully populated. The engine can chain a question from "MRSA × precaution type → Contact" into "Contact × PPE → Gown + gloves" using the sibling Concept.
- **Why this Concept and `isolation-precaution-types.md` both exist.** Two Concepts of the same content domain are allowed when the Rows differ materially. The sibling's Rows are precaution categories; this Concept's Rows are pathogens. NCLEX tests both directions: "patient has X — what precaution?" (this Concept) and "patient is on Contact Precautions — what PPE?" (sibling). Engine demos at the bottom illustrate how the two Concepts compose.
- **Pertussis duration trap.** Pertussis Droplet Precautions end **5 days** after initiation of effective antimicrobial therapy [s1], not after 24 hours. The 24-hour rule applies to Neisseria meningitidis, Group A streptococcus, and Haemophilus influenzae type b — distinct organisms not in this 10-pathogen set; SME may add. Wrong-answer pattern: discontinuing pertussis precautions at 24 hours.
- **TB skin-test positive without active disease.** A patient with a positive PPD or IGRA but no signs of active pulmonary disease requires only Standard Precautions [s1]. This Concept's TB Row covers active pulmonary or laryngeal TB; latent TB infection is out of scope and intentionally not a separate Row.
- **Varicella vs measles vs TB.** All three are airborne. Varicella adds Contact Precautions because of vesicular skin lesions, hence Contact + Airborne [s1]. Measles and TB are Airborne only. Wrong-answer pattern: omitting gown and gloves for varicella, or assigning Contact + Airborne to measles.
- **MRSA route is contact, not droplet, even in pneumonia.** Precaution category tracks the organism's transmission route, not the body site. MRSA pneumonia is still Contact [s1]. Wrong-answer pattern: assigning Droplet for MRSA pneumonia.
- **C. difficile hand hygiene exception.** Soap and water is preferred over alcohol-based handrub because alcohol does not kill C. difficile spores [s1]. The sibling `isolation-precaution-types.md` Contact Row's hand-hygiene cell does not call out this exception per organism — it is a per-pathogen Fact and lives here.
- **Norovirus mask exception.** Personnel cleaning areas heavily contaminated with feces or vomitus may wear a mask because norovirus can be aerosolized from those substances during cleaning [s1]. This is an environmental-cleaning-task exception under Contact Precautions, not a category change to Droplet.
- **COVID-19 row tracks current CDC guidance, not the 2007 baseline.** The 2007 Appendix A predates SARS-CoV-2; current CDC SARS-CoV-2 IPC recommendations [s2] place SARS-CoV-2 under Standard plus Contact + Droplet with N95 and AIIR for aerosol-generating procedures. NCLEX answers should track current CDC guidance. SME should re-verify against the live CDC SARS-CoV-2 IPC page on next pass.
- **Out of scope for this Concept.** PPE donning and doffing sequence (sibling: `ppe-donning-doffing-sequence.md`). Hand-hygiene 5 moments (sibling: `hand-hygiene-five-moments.md`). Sterile field maintenance and surgical asepsis (siblings: `sterile-field-maintenance.md`, `surgical-vs-medical-asepsis.md`). Chain of infection (sibling: `chain-of-infection.md`). The full list of CDC Appendix A entries (Neisseria meningitidis, Group A streptococcus, RSV, mumps, rubella, herpes zoster localized vs disseminated, smallpox, plague, viral hemorrhagic fevers, scabies, pediculosis, etc.) — SME may expand Rows in a follow-up pass.

### Tricky distractors

- **Droplet vs Airborne for respiratory pathogens.** Influenza and pertussis are Droplet (surgical mask, single-patient room without special air handling). TB and measles are Airborne (N95, AIIR). Wrong-answer pattern: assigning N95 to a pertussis patient or surgical mask to a TB patient.
- **MRSA / VRE colonization vs active infection.** Both require Contact Precautions for colonized patients, not just actively infected ones [s1]. Wrong-answer pattern: discontinuing precautions because the wound culture cleared while the patient remains colonized.
- **C. difficile vs norovirus hand hygiene.** Both prefer soap and water in their precaution cells, but for different reasons: C. difficile because alcohol does not kill spores; norovirus because soap-and-water is more effective at removing the non-enveloped virus from hands [s1]. Either way, soap and water is the right answer for both.
- **Varicella duration — "all lesions crusted and dry."** Not "no fever," not "rash gone," not "24 hours on antiviral." The dry-and-crusted criterion is the tested endpoint [s1].
- **COVID-19 vs influenza precaution type.** Influenza is Droplet only [s1]. COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2) is Contact + Droplet with N95 + AIIR layered for aerosol-generating procedures per current CDC guidance [s2]. Wrong-answer pattern: assigning Droplet only to a COVID-19 patient or assigning Contact + Droplet to a seasonal influenza patient.
- **Pertussis vs N. meningitidis duration.** Both are Droplet bacterial pathogens treated with antibiotics, but pertussis is 5 days post-therapy initiation while N. meningitidis is 24 hours post-therapy initiation [s1]. (N. meningitidis is not a Row in this Concept; the trap is misremembering pertussis as 24 hours.)

- `Pertussis | duration of precautions → ?` → Until 5 days after initiation of effective antimicrobial therapy
- `Varicella | duration of precautions → ?` → Until all lesions are crusted and dry
