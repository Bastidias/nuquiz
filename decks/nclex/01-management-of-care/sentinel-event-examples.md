# Sentinel / Never Event Examples

**Topic:** 1 — Management of Care &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 1.15, 1.14, IP-CD
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

Five canonical sentinel / never events the NCLEX-RN tests under sub-objectives 1.15 (Performance Improvement / Quality Improvement) and 1.14 (Legal Rights and Responsibilities). Rows are individual events; columns are the regulatory category each event falls under, the mandatory action the facility owes after the event, and the reporting timeline that attaches to that action. The Concept's purpose is to expose shared Values across Rows — every event in the list shares the same Joint Commission analysis-and-action-plan timeline (45 business days), and several share the comprehensive systematic analysis / root cause analysis mandatory action — so the engine can probe both per-Row recall and shared-Value cross-Row select-all. Sibling Concept `incident-report-rules.md` (1.14, 1.15) covers the incident-report mechanics; this Concept covers the event taxonomy.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Row name decision: `Stage 3+ pressure injury`.** The NCSBN-style label "Severe pressure injury (stage 3+)" carries a parenthetical staging detail. Renamed to `Stage 3+ pressure injury` (the canonical NPIAP / AHRQ / CMS construction — CMS lists "Pressure ulcers, stages III & IV" as a single HAC category [s5], and AHRQ PSNet describes "stage 3, stage 4, and unstageable pressure ulcers" as the never-event subset [s7]). The `+` covers stage 3, stage 4, and unstageable / deep tissue injury, which CMS treats as the severe-pressure-injury bundle. This eliminates the parenthetical and keeps the canonical staging language. Considered (b) keep parens — rejected because the parenthetical would smuggle staging into the cell despite no shared-Value violation. Considered (c) move staging to its own Column — rejected because with five Rows the column would carry only one populated cell (Aspects-style exception), which Dimensions Concepts should avoid per `knowledge-map.md`.
- **Sentinel event vs Never event vs HAC.** These are three overlapping but distinct US regulatory frames. **Sentinel event** is The Joint Commission's term for a patient safety event reaching the patient that results in death, permanent harm, or severe temporary harm; the accreditation-focused frame [s4]. **Never event** is the National Quality Forum's term (originally introduced by Ken Kizer in 2001 and now framed as Serious Reportable Events) for adverse events that are unambiguous, serious, and usually preventable [s1]. **Hospital-Acquired Condition (HAC)** is CMS's payment-policy frame under the Deficit Reduction Act of 2005 §5001(c); CMS does not pay the higher MS-DRG when one of the listed conditions is not present on admission [s5]. An event can carry one, two, or all three labels — wrong-site surgery is sentinel + NQF + CMS-nonpayment; retained foreign object is sentinel + NQF + HAC; patient suicide is sentinel + NQF (no CMS payment penalty since it isn't on the HAC list); stage 3+ pressure injury is NQF + HAC and crosses into sentinel territory only when the harm threshold is met [s7].
- **CMS HAC list (the 10 categories under §5001(c)).** As context for the `category` column: foreign object retained after surgery, air embolism, blood incompatibility, stage III/IV pressure ulcers, falls and trauma, manifestations of poor glycemic control, catheter-associated UTI, vascular catheter-associated infection, surgical site infection following CABG / bariatric / orthopedic procedures, and DVT/PE following total knee or hip replacement [s5]. Effective October 1, 2008. Two Rows in this Concept (retained foreign object, stage 3+ pressure injury) appear on this list directly; wrong-site surgery is treated separately by CMS via the National Coverage Determination withdrawing payment as of February 2009 [s2].
- **45 business days, not calendar days.** The Joint Commission timeline is 45 *business* days from the date the organization became aware of the event [s4]. Common confusion-point: "45 days" without the "business" qualifier.
- **Reporting to The Joint Commission is voluntary; conducting the analysis is not.** A facility is not required to report a sentinel event to TJC, but accredited organizations must conduct the comprehensive systematic analysis (root cause analysis) and develop an action plan whether reported or not [s4]. State-mandated reporting laws vary and may impose additional reporting; this Concept captures the federal / accreditation floor.
- **Patient-suicide window.** Joint Commission's reviewable sentinel-event definition for suicide includes events occurring during treatment AND within 72 hours of discharge from a staffed care setting [s3]. Common exam confusion-point: assuming the 72-hour window doesn't count.
- **Cross-Concept links.** Sibling `incident-report-rules.md` (1.14, 1.15) covers the internal incident-report workflow that surfaces sentinel events for review. Sibling `quality-improvement-cycle.md` (1.15) covers the PDSA cycle that the resulting action plan typically runs through. Sibling `tort-categories-in-nursing.md` (1.14) covers the tort framing — sentinel events frequently anchor malpractice claims, but the tort frame is separate from the patient-safety frame.
- **Out of scope.** State-specific mandatory reporting laws (state variance is real but NCLEX tests the predominant US framework — Joint Commission + CMS + NQF). The full NQF Serious Reportable Events list of seven categories (surgical, product/device, patient protection, care management, environmental, radiologic, criminal) is captured in Notes for context but only the five canonical NCLEX exemplars are Rows here. Specific RCA tools (fishbone, 5-whys, Ishikawa) belong with PDSA / QI Concepts.

### Tricky distractors

- **Sentinel vs Never event.** The two terms overlap but are not synonyms. NCLEX items sometimes treat them interchangeably, and pedagogically that's defensible for the five Rows above — but the engine can probe the distinction by hiding `category` and asking the student to identify which Row carries which label. Wrong-answer pattern: claiming all sentinel events are CMS-nonpayment events. They aren't — patient suicide is a sentinel event but not on the CMS HAC list.
- **45 days vs 45 business days.** The Joint Commission policy explicitly specifies *business* days. Wrong-answer pattern: "Within 45 days" without the qualifier.
- **Suicide timing window.** Suicide occurring during care is sentinel; suicide within 72 hours of discharge is also sentinel under the Joint Commission's reviewable-event definition [s3]. Wrong-answer pattern: "Sentinel only if it happens in the hospital."
- **Stage 3+ pressure injury severity threshold.** Stage 1 and 2 pressure injuries are reportable internally and HAC-tracked when severe, but only stage 3, stage 4, and unstageable pressure injuries carry the never-event / severe-HAC designation [s7]. Wrong-answer pattern: classifying a stage 2 hospital-acquired pressure injury as a sentinel event.
- **CMS payment effect vs Joint Commission analysis.** These are different consequences. CMS denies the higher MS-DRG payment for the case [s5]; Joint Commission requires analysis and action plan [s4]. Wrong-answer pattern: conflating "no payment" with "report to Joint Commission" as the same mandatory action.
- **Wrong-site surgery prevention vs response.** The Universal Protocol (site marking, time-out, verification) [s2] is the *prevention* mandate; root cause analysis is the *response* mandate after an event occurs. This Concept's `mandatory action` column captures response, not prevention. Wrong-answer pattern: listing "Universal Protocol" as the mandatory action after wrong-site surgery — by then it has already been bypassed.

- `? | reporting timeline → Action plan submitted to Joint Commission within 45 business days` → Wrong-site surgery, Retained foreign object, Patient suicide in care, Medication error causing death (cross-Row select-all, shared-Value detection across four Rows).
- `? | mandatory action → Comprehensive systematic analysis` → Wrong-site surgery, Retained foreign object, Patient suicide in care, Medication error causing death (cross-Row select-all).
- `? | mandatory action → Root cause analysis` → all five Rows (cross-Row select-all spanning every event in the table).
- `Patient suicide in care | category → ?` → Sentinel event / NQF Serious Reportable Event / Includes suicide within 72 hours of discharge.
- `Stage 3+ pressure injury | reporting timeline → ?` → CMS payment denied for case / Action plan submitted to Joint Commission within 45 business days when severe harm or death.
- `? | category → CMS Hospital-Acquired Condition` → Retained foreign object, Stage 3+ pressure injury (cross-Row select-all, two-Row pairing).
- Composite Row profile: Patient suicide in care across all Columns, with the `category` cell swapped to `CMS Hospital-Acquired Condition` (a Stage 3+ pressure injury Value) — tests whether the student knows suicide isn't on the CMS HAC list.
