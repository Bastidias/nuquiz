# Restraint Use Rules

**Topic:** 1 — Management of Care &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 1.5, 1.14, IP-CD, IP-CJ
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

Federal restraint and seclusion rules from 42 CFR 482.13(e) — the CMS Conditions of Participation that every Medicare-participating hospital follows. Rows distinguish the type of intervention (physical non-violent, chemical, seclusion, behavioral-emergency restraint) and split the behavioral-emergency Row into three age tiers because the federal regulation assigns a different `max order duration` to each tier (4 hours adult, 2 hours age 9–17, 1 hour under 9). Splitting by age tier instead of stuffing the three numbers into one cell avoids a parenthetical-list violation and lets the engine detect that order requirement, monitoring, indication, and documentation are shared across the three behavioral-emergency Rows.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells. The Row labels for behavioral-emergency restraint carry an age-tier qualifier (`adult age 18+`, `age 9 through 17`, `under age 9`) — this is Row scoping, analogous to splitting a Concept across three Rows where one cell value differs (max order duration) but the rest are shared.
- **Why behavioral-emergency restraint is split into three Rows.** 42 CFR 482.13(e) assigns a different time-limit ceiling to each age tier — 4 hours, 2 hours, 1 hour. Putting all three numbers in one cell would smuggle `age group` into a parenthetical and break atomicity (any answer-option built from that cell would force the student to know all three at once, defeating gap diagnostics). Splitting by Row keeps each `max order duration` cell as a single atomic Fact AND lets the engine detect that order requirement, monitoring, indication, and documentation Values are *shared* across all three age tiers (cross-Row select-all becomes possible).
- **Why seclusion is its own Row, not folded into behavioral-emergency restraint.** 42 CFR 482.13(e) defines seclusion separately ("involuntary confinement of a patient alone in a room or area from which the patient is physically prevented from leaving") and limits its use to violent/self-destructive behavior management — the same indication as behavioral-emergency restraint. Time limits and monitoring are identical. Splitting into a separate Row preserves the definitional distinction while exposing the shared cells (indication, order requirement, monitoring, documentation).
- **Physical restraint definition.** 42 CFR 482.13(e)(1)(i)(A) defines physical restraint as "any manual method, physical or mechanical device, material, or equipment that immobilizes or reduces the ability of a patient to move his or her arms, legs, body, or head freely" [s1]. The non-violent/non-self-destructive Row covers situations where physical restraint is used to prevent a patient from interfering with treatment (e.g., pulling at lines, falling out of bed) without violent intent.
- **Chemical restraint definition.** A medication is a "drug used as a restraint" only if it is used to manage the patient's behavior or restrict freedom of movement AND is not a standard treatment or dosage for the patient's condition [s1]. PRN antipsychotics for an agitated patient with no history of psychosis: chemical restraint. Standing-dose haloperidol for diagnosed schizophrenia: not chemical restraint.
- **The 1-hour face-to-face rule.** For violent/self-destructive restraint or seclusion, a physician, other licensed practitioner, or trained registered nurse must see the patient face-to-face within 1 hour of restraint initiation [s1]. The trained-RN option is contingent on the RN consulting with the attending physician as soon as possible after the evaluation. This applies only to the violent/self-destructive scenarios — non-violent restraint has no 1-hour rule.
- **No PRN, no standing orders.** Across all restraint types, federal rules forbid PRN ("as needed") and standing orders [s1]. Each restraint episode requires its own time-limited order. The "PRN restraint" wrong-answer pattern is a perennial NCLEX trap.
- **Death reporting (out of scope for this Concept, sibling for later).** 42 CFR 482.13(g) requires hospitals to report to CMS deaths associated with restraint or seclusion within specific timeframes [s1]. Sibling Concept for `sentinel-event-examples.md` (T1 §1.15) and any future patient-safety reporting Concept; flagged here so the cross-link is visible.
- **State-law floor vs ceiling.** Federal rules are the floor. State law and hospital policy can be more restrictive (shorter time limits, narrower indication, more frequent monitoring). NCLEX answers default to the federal rule unless the stem says otherwise.

### Tricky distractors

- **Non-violent vs violent restraint indication.** The federal hour ceilings (4/2/1) apply ONLY to violent or self-destructive behavior management. Non-violent physical restraint (e.g., bilateral soft wrist restraints to prevent an elderly delirious patient from pulling out an NG tube) is NOT subject to those hour caps — renewal cadence is set by hospital policy. Wrong-answer pattern: applying the 4-hour adult cap to a non-violent restraint scenario.
- **The 1-hour face-to-face is for the patient, not the order.** Students sometimes confuse the 1-hour face-to-face evaluation (assessment of the patient within 1 hour of initiation) with a 1-hour order limit. The 4/2/1-hour numbers are MAX ORDER DURATION; the 1-hour face-to-face is a SEPARATE evaluation deadline.
- **Trained RN can do the 1-hour face-to-face.** Common misconception: only an MD can perform the evaluation. The federal rule allows a physician, other licensed practitioner, OR a trained RN to do it [s1]. The trained-RN option still requires consulting the attending physician as soon as possible afterward.
- **Chemical restraint vs PRN psych medication.** A PRN order for haloperidol on a patient with chronic schizophrenia for agitation related to psychosis is treatment, not restraint. The same dose given to a delirious post-op patient with no psychotic-disorder history specifically to "calm them down" is chemical restraint. The discriminator is "standard treatment / standard dosage for the patient's condition" [s1].
- **Seclusion is not time-out.** Time-out is voluntary and the patient can leave; seclusion is involuntary confinement and the patient cannot leave [s1]. Wrong-answer pattern: treating "the patient was placed in their room with the door closed but not locked, and asked to stay there to calm down" as seclusion.
- **24-hour ceiling on consecutive orders.** After 24 hours of continuous restraint or seclusion (regardless of how many renewal orders), the physician or licensed practitioner must see and assess the patient in person before issuing a new order [s1]. Telephone renewal cannot extend past 24 hours.

## Engine demo opportunities

- `Seclusion | indication → ?` → Management of violent or self-destructive behavior that jeopardizes physical safety
- `Behavioral emergency restraint — adult age 18+ | max order duration → ?` → 4 hours, 24-hour cap before in-person re-evaluation for new order (multi-Fact cell)
- `Behavioral emergency restraint — age 9 through 17 | max order duration → ?` → 2 hours
- `Behavioral emergency restraint — under age 9 | max order duration → ?` → 1 hour
- `? | order requirement → 1-hour face-to-face evaluation by physician, licensed practitioner, or trained RN` → Seclusion, Behavioral emergency restraint — adult age 18+, Behavioral emergency restraint — age 9 through 17, Behavioral emergency restraint — under age 9 (cross-Row select-all, isolates the four violent/self-destructive Rows)
- `? | monitoring frequency → Continuous in-person observation` → Seclusion, Behavioral emergency restraint — adult age 18+, Behavioral emergency restraint — age 9 through 17, Behavioral emergency restraint — under age 9 (same four)
- `Physical (non-violent / non-self-destructive) | max order duration → ?` → Renewal per hospital policy, No federal hour cap (multi-Fact cell — distinguishes non-violent from violent restraint)
- `? | order requirement → No PRN orders` → all six Rows (cross-Row select-all, federal-rule shared Value)
- Composite Row profile: Behavioral emergency restraint — under age 9, with the `max order duration` cell swapped to `4 hours` (an adult Value) — tests the age-tier discrimination.
