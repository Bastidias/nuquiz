# Interdisciplinary Team Members

**Topic:** 1 — Management of Care &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 1.6, 1.16, IP-CD
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

The non-nursing-team disciplines a bedside RN coordinates with on a typical adult inpatient unit, plus the RN row as the coordination anchor. NCLEX-RN sub-objectives 1.6 (Collaboration with Interdisciplinary Team) and 1.16 (Referrals) test the candidate's ability to match a patient finding to the discipline best suited to address it — the `typical referral trigger` column carries the highest exam yield. The framework is anchored in AHRQ TeamSTEPPS, which identifies team-based care as a structure of clearly defined roles operating through communication, leadership, situation monitoring, and mutual support [s1]. Discipline-specific scope content draws from peer-reviewed primary literature where canonical NCSBN/AHRQ sources do not enumerate per-role triggers; cells without a single canonical citation are marked in the unsourced-values table.

**Layout convention:** rows are roles ordered by typical bedside frequency on a medical-surgical unit (RN as coordination anchor, then prescribing roles MD/DO and NP/PA, then rehabilitation disciplines RT, PT, OT, SLP, then RD and SW for whole-patient needs, then Pharmacist, then Chaplain). Columns progress left → right from terse identifying scope to the most exam-tested column (typical referral trigger). Each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Canonical-name exemption: MD/DO and NP/PA.** Both slashed forms are state-nomenclature variants of the same prescribing role. MD (Doctor of Medicine, allopathic) and DO (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine) hold equivalent unrestricted licenses to diagnose, prescribe, and perform procedures in all 50 US states; NCLEX-RN treats their scope as identical for nursing-team coordination purposes. NP (Nurse Practitioner) and PA (Physician Assistant) have different educational paths but operate with comparable inpatient scope on most US acute-care services [s2]. Treated as single Rows each, exempt from the comma-list atomization rule (same exemption that applies to LPN/VN and UAP/AP in sibling Concept `scope-of-practice-by-role.md`).
- **Role-abbreviation expansions for Row labels.** The Row labels use clinical abbreviations as published in NCLEX-RN content. Expansions: RT = Respiratory Therapist [s3]; PT = Physical Therapist [s4]; OT = Occupational Therapist [s5]; SLP = Speech-Language Pathologist [s6]; RD = Registered Dietitian (also Registered Dietitian Nutritionist, RDN) [s7]; SW = Social Worker [s8]. NP = Nurse Practitioner; PA = Physician Assistant; MD = Doctor of Medicine; DO = Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine.
- **Why the `typical referral trigger` column is the highest-yield NCLEX column.** NCSBN sub-objective 1.16 (Referrals) is folded into 1.6 in this Concept per Topic 1 README. NCLEX-RN items typically present a patient finding ("the patient coughs while drinking water"; "the patient cannot dress without assistance"; "the patient screens positive for malnutrition") and ask the candidate to identify the discipline to consult. The `typical referral trigger` column is structured to make this matching mechanical: each trigger is a finding the bedside RN observes or screens for.
- **What this Concept does not cover.** Nursing-team roles (RN scope vs LPN/VN vs UAP/AP) live in sibling Concept `scope-of-practice-by-role.md` (1.3). Care-coordination roles internal to nursing (case manager, discharge planner, care coordinator, utilization reviewer) live in sibling Concept `care-coordination-roles.md` (1.4). The handoff structure for inter-discipline communication lives in sibling Concept `sbar-handoff-structure.md` (1.6). This Concept covers only the non-nursing-team disciplines plus RN as the coordination anchor.
- **RN row as coordination anchor.** The RN row is included so the engine can generate cross-Row select-all questions (`? | when consulted → Admission` returns RN, MD/DO, RD, Pharmacist) and shared-Value detection. Removing it would make the table a list of consultees with no consult-er.
- **AHRQ TeamSTEPPS scope.** TeamSTEPPS is the AHRQ/DOD evidence-based framework for team-based care; it defines the *structure* (clearly-defined roles, four core skills) and is sourced for the RN coordination row and the general team-based-care framing [s1]. TeamSTEPPS does not enumerate per-discipline referral triggers — those come from discipline-specific peer-reviewed literature.
- **State and facility variance.** NP/PA prescribing authority varies by state (full practice vs. reduced vs. restricted) and facility privileging; the cell value reflects the predominant US acute-care convention that NCLEX-RN tests [s2]. Pharmacist independent prescribing authority similarly varies by state and is not tested as a default-yes scope on NCLEX-RN.

### Tricky distractors

- **PT vs OT.** Wrong-answer pattern: confusing PT (mobility, gait, lower-body function) with OT (ADLs, upper-body function, adaptive equipment). The discriminator is whether the deficit is *getting somewhere* (PT) or *doing something once you arrive* (OT). A patient who cannot walk is PT; a patient who can walk but cannot dress is OT.
- **SLP vs RD on dysphagia.** Wrong-answer pattern: consulting RD for a coughing-while-drinking patient. RD addresses *what* the patient should eat once swallowing is established; SLP addresses *whether and how* the patient can swallow safely. SLP comes first; RD follows with the diet-texture recommendation SLP cleared.
- **Social worker vs case manager.** Wrong-answer pattern: assuming SW and case manager are interchangeable. Both touch discharge planning, but SW focuses on psychosocial barriers (housing, financial, abuse, family conflict) and community-resource linkage [s8]; case manager focuses on care-pathway coordination and utilization. See sibling `care-coordination-roles.md` (1.4) for the case-manager scope.
- **Chaplain referral is patient-driven.** Wrong-answer pattern: assuming chaplains are consulted only at end of life. Chaplains are also referred for spiritual distress at any point in admission, advance-care-planning conversations, and family support during difficult diagnoses [s11]. Patient or family request is itself a sufficient trigger.
- **RT vs RN for routine oxygen titration.** Wrong-answer pattern: paging RT for every oxygen change. RT is consulted for ventilator management, bronchodilator administration, complex airway concerns, and tracheostomy care; routine nasal-cannula titration on a stable patient is within RN scope. The distinguishing feature is complexity and equipment.
- **Pharmacist referral is not "every medication question."** Wrong-answer pattern: paging the pharmacist for any medication query. Pharmacists are consulted for medication reconciliation at transitions, polypharmacy, suspected drug interactions, renal- or hepatic-dose adjustments, and high-risk regimens [s9]. Routine drug-information questions can be answered from the drug reference; pharmacist consult is reserved for clinical complexity.
