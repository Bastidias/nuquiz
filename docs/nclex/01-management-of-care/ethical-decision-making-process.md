# Ethical Decision-Making Process

**Topic:** 1 — Management of Care &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Ordered &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 1.11, IP-CJ
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

The sequential process a nurse follows to work through a clinical ethics dilemma — recognize the dilemma, gather facts and stakeholders, generate and weigh options, act, and evaluate the result. NCSBN's 2026 RN test plan §1.11 (Ethical Practice) requires the candidate to "Recognize and report ethical dilemmas," "Inform client and staff members of ethical issues affecting client care," "Practice in a manner consistent with the nurses' code of ethics," and "Evaluate outcomes of interventions to promote ethical practice" `[s5]` — those four activity statements span the full cycle this Concept lays out. Multiple published nursing-ethics models converge on the same shape: the Nursing Process / ADPIE adaptation `[s3]`, the MORAL mnemonic `[s1, s3]`, the PLUS seven-step model `[s1, s3]`, the Jonsen/Siegler/Winslade Four-Quadrant approach `[s1, s3]`, and Park's integrated six-step model `[s2]` all share the assess-options-act-evaluate skeleton. This Concept names the seven canonical steps; sibling Concept `ethical-principles.md` covers the principles (autonomy, beneficence, etc.) the nurse applies inside the Evaluate-options step.

**Layout convention:** rows are the seven decision-making steps in canonical order. Columns progress left → right from what the nurse does at this step (Action), through the supporting resource the nurse reaches for (Tool/resource), to what goes wrong if the step is skipped or rushed (Failure mode). Each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Synthesis-by-direct-derivation disclosure.** The seven-step row sequence is a synthesis across multiple published models — each row name appears in at least one of the canonical frameworks (Nursing Process / MORAL / PLUS / Four-Quadrant / Park integrated model), but no single source labels its steps with these exact seven names. Action, Tool/resource, and Failure-mode cells are sourced to the published model(s) that articulate that step's content; cells citing multiple sources reflect cross-model convergence rather than per-source repetition. This follows the SKILL's synthesis-by-direct-derivation rule: cite the primary models that justify each cell, disclose the row-naming synthesis here, do not mark cells `[needs source]`.
- **Cross-Concept link — `ethical-principles.md`.** This Concept is the *process*; `ethical-principles.md` is the Dimensions Concept of the *principles* (autonomy, beneficence, nonmaleficence, justice, fidelity, veracity) that the nurse applies inside step 5 (Evaluate options). The principle list is referenced here as Tool/resource cells; full definitions and dilemma examples live there.
- **Cross-Concept link — `ethics-committee-and-irb.md`** (not yet authored). Ethics committees are referenced as a Tool/resource in steps 3, 5, and 7. A future Concept can compare ethics committee vs. IRB vs. ethics consult service.
- **Model name shortcuts.**
  - **MORAL** = Massage the dilemma → Outline options → Review and resolve → Affirm and act → Look back `[s1, s3]`.
  - **PLUS** filters = Policies / Laws / Universal values / Self-identification of fairness `[s1, s3]`.
  - **Four-Quadrant** (Jonsen/Siegler/Winslade) = Health-care indications, Client/family preferences, Quality of life, Contextual features `[s1, s3]`.
  - **Park integrated** = six steps spanning identify problem → collect information → develop alternatives → select with justification → implement → evaluate and prevent recurrence `[s2]`.
- **Distinct from the nursing process (ADPIE).** ADPIE governs care for one client (Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation). The ethical decision-making process governs how the nurse navigates a moral conflict — same iterative shape, different content. NCSBN 1.11 items test the ethics process; clinical-judgment items use ADPIE / NCJMM.
- **Distinct from PDSA.** PDSA (sibling `quality-improvement-cycle.md`) is a unit-level process-improvement cycle; this Concept is a per-dilemma decision process. Both are iterative, both have an evaluate step, but the scope (one ethical case vs. one care-process change) is different.
- **Out of scope.** Specific ANA Code provision numbers, the full text of the Four-Quadrant questions per quadrant, and ethics-committee composition rules are not modeled here — they belong in their own Concepts if SME wants depth on any of them.

### Tricky distractors

- **Identify dilemma vs Gather data.** Wrong-answer pattern: starting data collection before naming the conflict. Recognizing that *an* ethical conflict exists is step 1; the *details* of the conflict are gathered in step 2.
- **Gather data vs Identify stakeholders.** Wrong-answer pattern: treating the client's preferences as one more chart fact. Stakeholder elicitation is its own step because preferences shift the option set, not just the data set.
- **Identify options vs Evaluate options.** Wrong-answer pattern: applying ethical principles before listing alternatives. Generating options is divergent (open the field); evaluating is convergent (narrow to one).
- **Implement vs Evaluate outcome.** Wrong-answer pattern: ending the cycle at action. Without the Evaluate step the same dilemma recurs and moral residue accumulates `[s1, s3]`.
- **Ethical decision-making process vs Ethical principles.** Wrong-answer pattern: substituting "Beneficence" or "Autonomy" for one of the seven steps. Principles are *what the nurse applies* (sibling Concept); the steps are *what the nurse does*.
