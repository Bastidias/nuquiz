# Never Events (Clinical Category)

**Topic:** 2 — Safety and Infection Prevention and Control &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 2.1, 1.15, 7-cross-tag, IP-CJ
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

Six canonical never events the NCLEX-RN tests under sub-objective 2.1 (Accident / Error / Injury Prevention). Rows are individual events; columns are the regulatory category each event falls under, the mandatory action the facility owes after the event, the reporting timeline that attaches to that action, and the prevention bundle the bedside nurse executes to stop the event from happening. The Concept's purpose is to expose the **prevention** angle that the T1 sibling `sentinel-event-examples.md` (governance / QI framing) deliberately excludes. Sibling carries the same Rows; this Concept shifts the load from response (RCA, action plan, 45-business-day timeline) to bedside clinical practice (Universal Protocol, surgical counts, two-identifier verification, barcode med admin, repositioning bundle, suicide-precaution rounds). Cross-reference T1 sibling for the regulatory-response framing.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Row name decision: `Hospital-acquired stage 3+ pressure injury`.** The Topic 2 README spec wrote this Row as `Hospital-acquired pressure injury (stage 3+)`. Renamed (option a) to put the staging qualifier inline rather than parenthetical. Rationale matches the T1 sibling `sentinel-event-examples.md` rename: the parenthetical would smuggle staging into the cell, doesn't share with another Row, and CMS / AHRQ PSNet language treats "stages III & IV" / "stage 3, stage 4, and unstageable" as a single severity bundle [s5, s7]. Considered (b) keep parens — rejected per knowledge-map atomicity rule. Considered (c) split staging into its own Column — rejected because only this Row carries staging detail (the other five Rows have no analogous staging qualifier), making the column 5/6 empty.
- **Distinction from `sentinel-event-examples` (T1, governance / QI framing).** That sibling Concept carries five of these six Rows (it omits Wrong-patient procedure) and exposes the same three Columns for `category`, `mandatory action`, `reporting timeline`. This Concept is the **clinical / bedside framing** — it adds the sixth Row (Wrong-patient procedure, since identification errors are a Topic 2 staple) and adds the `prevention bundle` Column. The first three Columns are reproduced verbatim from the sibling so the engine can match shared Values across both Concepts during a future merge if SME consolidates. Whether to merge is flagged in the Topic 2 README; both Concepts coexist for now.
- **Universal Protocol vs prevention bundle.** The Universal Protocol is the umbrella name for three components: preprocedure verification, site marking, and time-out [s2]. The cell breaks it into atomic Facts so each component is independently distractable and gradable. Same approach for surgical counts (initial / additions / pre-closure are three separable counts) and for medication-administration prevention (rights, two identifiers, barcode patient, barcode medication, double-check, smart pump are six separable practices).
- **Joint Commission monitoring level for inpatient suicide.** Joint Commission FAQs specify continuous 1:1 observation (one qualified staff member to one patient at all times, including while the patient sleeps, toilets, or bathes) for patients identified as high-risk for suicide in units with ligature or other safety risks; video monitoring alone is not acceptable as a stand-alone intervention [s8]. Lower-risk observation tiers (line-of-sight, q15-minute checks) exist in clinical practice but are not the Joint Commission floor for high-risk; the Concept reflects the high-risk floor. Frequency-of-rounds detail belongs in a future T4 / T7 suicide-precautions Concept.
- **45 business days, not calendar days.** Same as T1 sibling — TJC timeline is 45 *business* days from the date the organization became aware of the event [s4]. Common confusion-point.
- **Wrong-patient procedure scope.** The wrong-patient row in this Concept covers procedures generally (surgery, biopsy, transfusion, medication administration). NCLEX 2.1 tests two-identifier verification across all of these. Wrong-patient surgery specifically falls under the Universal Protocol [s2]; wrong-patient transfusion / medication / specimen labeling fall under the broader two-identifier rule [s10]. The cell consolidates the prevention floor that applies across all of them.
- **Medication-error severity threshold.** Only medication errors **causing death** are NQF Serious Reportable Events [s1]; medication errors causing temporary or permanent harm are typically tracked as adverse drug events but don't all rise to never-event status. Wrong-answer pattern: classifying every medication error as a never event.
- **Cross-Concept links.** T1 sibling `sentinel-event-examples.md` (1.14, 1.15) covers governance / regulatory-response framing for five of the six Rows. T1 sibling `incident-report-rules.md` (1.14, 1.15) covers the internal incident-report workflow that surfaces these events for review. T2 sibling `pressure-injury-staging.md` (2.1, 5-cross-tag) carries the staging definitions referenced in the stage 3+ Row. T2 sibling `fall-risk-factors-and-mitigations.md` (2.1) covers a related Topic-2 Accident / Injury Prevention Concept. Future T7 (Reduction of Risk Potential) Concepts will likely re-Tag this Concept with a real `7.x` ID once the T7 README is authored — `7-cross-tag` is the placeholder per `tags.md`.
- **Out of scope.** State-specific mandatory reporting laws (state variance is real but NCLEX tests the predominant US framework). The full NQF Serious Reportable Events list of seven categories is captured in the T1 sibling Notes for context. Specific RCA tools (fishbone, 5-whys) belong with PDSA / QI Concepts in T1. Prevention bundles for non-never-event safety topics (e.g., CAUTI, CLABSI, falls without serious harm) belong with their own Concepts.

### Tricky distractors

- **Universal Protocol is prevention, not response.** Same canonical confusion as the T1 sibling: the Universal Protocol (site marking, time-out, verification) [s2] is the *prevention* mandate. After a wrong-site event, the mandatory action is RCA + action plan [s1, s2, s3]. Wrong-answer pattern: listing "Universal Protocol" as the mandatory action after wrong-site surgery — by then it has already been bypassed.
- **Surgical counts: who counts, when.** The circulating nurse leads the count [s9], not the scrub tech and not the surgeon. Counts occur at three (not one) points: initial setup, additions during the procedure, and before wound closure [s9]. Wrong-answer pattern: "the scrub nurse performs the final count" or "one count at end of case."
- **Two patient identifiers vs barcode.** Two identifiers (typically name + date of birth) is the universal standard [s10]; barcode scanning is one technological implementation that confirms the wristband-to-medication match [s10]. Wrong-answer pattern: "barcode scanning replaces the two-identifier rule" — it doesn't, it implements it.
- **Independent double-check is independent.** Two nurses checking together is not independent; the second nurse independently verifies the medication, dose, route, and patient without seeing the first nurse's check [s10]. Wrong-answer pattern: "the two nurses verify together."
- **1:1 observation for high-suicide-risk is continuous.** Continuous 1:1 observation includes while the patient sleeps, toilets, and bathes [s8]. Video monitoring alone is not acceptable for high-risk patients with ligature access [s8]. Wrong-answer pattern: "video monitoring satisfies 1:1 observation."
- **Pressure-injury repositioning interval.** Source language describes "routine repositioning" with frequency individualized to tissue tolerance and risk [s11]; "every 2 hours" is the widely-taught nursing pedagogy default but is not stated as a fixed AHRQ requirement. The cell uses the AHRQ-sourced phrasing "Routine repositioning" rather than the q2h pedagogical default. Wrong-answer pattern items that hinge on a precise interval should be authored with care.
