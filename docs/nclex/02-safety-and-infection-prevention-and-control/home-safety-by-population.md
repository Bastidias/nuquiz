# Home Safety Hazards by Population

**Topic:** 2 — Safety and Infection Prevention and Control &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Dimensions &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 2.5, 3-cross-tag, IP-TL
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

Five life-stage rows × four shared attributes that NCSBN Topic 2.5 (Home Safety) [s1] tests through the Teaching/Learning lens (`IP-TL`). Rows are developmental populations because hazard pattern shifts predictably with developmental stage; columns are the four attributes a community/home-visit nurse uses to convert a population into a teaching encounter — top hazards (what is most likely to kill or injure), screening question (what to ask), intervention (what to do), and teaching focus (what to anchor the family/patient education on). The Concept overlaps Topic 3 anticipatory-guidance content; the cross-Tag `3-cross-tag` is a placeholder until Topic 3 README is populated.

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Row label `Infant/toddler`.** Slash exemption per the developmental-stage canonical pair pattern. CDC and AAP routinely group infants (0–12 months) and toddlers (1–3 years) for home-injury surveillance and anticipatory-guidance content because hazard mechanisms (sleep-related death, drowning in standing water, ingestion, falls from changing surfaces) span both. The slash is a single Row identifier, not a comma-list.
- **Why these five rows.** NCSBN's 2.5 framing is age-stratified home safety [s1]; CDC's child-injury and STEADI materials use exactly these life-stage breakpoints (under 5, 5–14, 15–19, working-age adult, 65+) for hazard data and intervention guidance [s2,s8]. Adolescent and Older adult rows are the densest because exam testing concentrates on the two highest-mortality groups — teen MVC/suicide and elder falls.
- **Row label `Older adult`.** Standard public-health term for adults 65+ used in CDC STEADI materials [s8] and in USPSTF age-cutoff statements [s9]. Not "geriatric" (clinical term, narrower) or "elderly" (deprecated in current CDC style).
- **Sub-objective overlap with Topic 3.** Anticipatory guidance by developmental stage is also home content for Topic 3 (Health Promotion and Maintenance). This Concept lives in T2 because the framing is hazard → intervention (safety lens); the T3 sibling, once authored, will frame the same age strata around developmental milestones and screening schedules. Cross-Tag is `3-cross-tag` (placeholder) per the tag registry.
- **Why `IP-TL`.** The `intervention` and `teaching focus` columns are dominated by patient/family education — the Teaching/Learning Integrated Process is the operative practice frame. `IP-CJ` (Clinical Judgment) is also defensible because the screening-question column is essentially cue recognition; not added to keep the Tag set focused on what the column structure most clearly emphasizes.
- **Cross-Concept links.** `home-oxygen-safety.md` (T2 §2.5) covers home oxygen as an Aspects Concept and is the natural sibling for the Adult and Older adult fire-safety angle. `fall-risk-factors-and-mitigations.md` (T2 §2.1) covers the eight individual fall-risk factors at finer granularity than the Older adult row's `top hazards` cell. `hospital-emergency-codes.md` and `fire-response-race-pass.md` (T2 §2.2) cover institutional fire response, not home fire response. Out of scope here: occupational hazards (workplace ergonomics is T2 §2.3), institutional fall bundles (covered in `fall-risk-factors-and-mitigations.md`), specific car-seat-by-weight tables.
- **Lethal-means counseling.** Listed under Adolescent intervention because firearm storage is the highest-yield modifiable means-restriction lever for adolescent suicide per CDC's risk/protective-factor framing [s5]. Also a teaching point for any Adult row when children or adolescents are in the home.
- **Three-key-questions screen.** The Older adult `screening question` cell uses STEADI's three-question short screen [s8] as the canonical screening tool; the longer 12-question Stay Independent screener is referenced under teaching focus. Sibling Concept `fall-risk-factors-and-mitigations.md` covers both screens at finer granularity.

### Tricky distractors

- **Infant supervision in water.** "Stay within sight" is wrong — the canonical CDC framing is **constant** supervision and arm's reach for infants and toddlers [s2]. Drowning happens quickly and quietly, even in standing water (toilets, buckets, bathtubs).
- **Pool fence specifications.** Four-sided fence at least four feet high with self-closing self-latching gate [s2]. Wrong-answer pattern: three-sided fence using the house wall as one side (CDC explicitly recommends four-sided isolation fencing because it doubles the protective effect).
- **Infant sleep surface.** "Firm flat noninclined" [s4]. Wrong-answer pattern: inclined sleepers (banned in 2019), couches, adult beds, car seats for routine sleep. AAP 2022 guidance explicitly added the "noninclined" qualifier.
- **Room-share vs bed-share.** Room-sharing is recommended; bed-sharing is not [s4]. Wrong-answer pattern: collapsing the two and recommending or warning against both.
- **Adolescent depression screen grade.** USPSTF Grade B for adolescents 12–18 [s6]. Wrong-answer pattern: claiming Grade A (overstates evidence) or claiming the same recommendation extends to children ≤11 (USPSTF rates that Grade I — insufficient evidence).
- **Suicide-risk screen grade.** USPSTF rates suicide-risk screening in children/adolescents as Grade I (insufficient evidence) [s6] — distinct from the Grade B for major depressive disorder screening, even though many depression instruments include a suicide-ideation item. Wrong-answer pattern: conflating the two.
- **Alcohol screening in adolescents.** USPSTF rates alcohol screening in adolescents as Grade I [s7]; the Grade B applies to adults 18+. Wrong-answer pattern: applying AUDIT-C universally to teens.
- **STEADI three-question screen.** "Fallen in past year? Feels unsteady? Worries about falling?" — YES to any flags risk [s8]. Wrong-answer pattern: requiring all three positive (overscreening miss).
