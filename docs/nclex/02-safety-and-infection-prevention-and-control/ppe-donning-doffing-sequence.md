# PPE Donning/Doffing Sequence

**Topic:** 2 — Safety and Infection Prevention and Control &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Pattern:** Ordered &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Tags:** 2.9, IP-CJ
**Status:** draft (SME review pending)

The order in which a nurse puts on and removes personal protective equipment is a tested NCLEX-RN safety procedure under sub-objective 2.9 (Standard Precautions / Transmission-Based Precautions / Surgical Asepsis). Donning order builds the barrier from inside out, ending with gloves over the gown cuff so no skin or clothing is exposed at the wrist. Doffing order reverses the contamination logic — the most contaminated item (gloves) comes off first, the respirator stays on longest because removing it requires touching near the face. The two sequences are inverses, and that inversion is the point students must recognize. This Concept presents them as two separate Ordered tables (Don and Doff) so each canonical sequence stays intact.

**Layout convention:** two Ordered tables — one for Don, one for Doff — each with rows in canonical step order. Columns progress left → right from the action performed (Action) to the published reason for that step's position (Rationale) to the most-tested mistake at that step (Common error). Each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hand hygiene | Perform hand hygiene before donning gloves [s4]<br>Use soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub [s4] | Hand hygiene before glove donning prevents transferring resident skin flora to the patient [s4]<br>Gloves are not a substitute for hand hygiene [s4] | Skipping hand hygiene because gloves will be worn [s4]<br>Donning PPE with hands not yet cleaned [needs source] |
| 2 | Gown | Cover torso from neck to knees [s1]<br>Cover arms to end of wrists [s1]<br>Wrap gown around the back [s1]<br>Fasten ties at back of neck [s1]<br>Fasten ties at waist [s1] | Gown forms the outermost body barrier and must be donned before items that would obstruct tying at the back [s1]<br>Inspect for tears or damage that reduce effectiveness before donning [s3] | Leaving the back open or loosely tied [needs source]<br>Pulling sleeves only to the forearm rather than the wrist [needs source] |
| 3 | Mask or respirator | Secure ties or elastic bands at middle of head and neck [s1]<br>Fit flexible band to nose bridge [s1]<br>Fit snug to face and below chin [s1]<br>Fit-check respirator [s1] | Mask is donned before goggles so the upper edge sits beneath the goggle frame and reduces eyewear fogging [needs source]<br>Fit-check on a respirator confirms seal before patient contact [s1] | Pinching the nose-bridge band loosely [needs source]<br>Skipping the respirator fit-check [s1] |
| 4 | Goggles or face shield | Place over face and eyes [s1]<br>Adjust to fit [s1] | Eye protection is added after the mask so the goggle frame seats over the mask's upper edge [needs source] | Wearing personal eyeglasses in place of goggles [needs source]<br>Goggles riding above the mask edge and leaving a gap [needs source] |

## Doff sequence

| # | Step | Action | Rationale | Common error |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gloves | Grasp outside of one glove with the opposite gloved hand [s1]<br>Peel the glove off [s1]<br>Hold the removed glove in the still-gloved hand [s1]<br>Slide fingers of the ungloved hand under the remaining glove at the wrist [s1]<br>Peel the second glove off over the first [s1]<br>Discard gloves in a waste container [s1] | Outside of gloves is contaminated [s1]<br>Outermost PPE is removed first because it is likely the most contaminated [s3]<br>Gloves come off first to prevent transferring contamination to other PPE items during their removal [s3] | Touching the outside of the second glove with bare skin [s1]<br>Snapping gloves off and aerosolizing contaminants [needs source] |
| 2 | Goggles or face shield | Handle by the headband or ear pieces only [s1]<br>Place in a designated receptacle for reprocessing or in a waste container [s1] | Outside of goggles or face shield is contaminated [s1]<br>The headband and ear pieces are the "clean" zones because they sit behind the head, away from splash exposure [s1] | Touching the front of the face shield while removing [s3]<br>Pulling goggles forward over the face instead of lifting from behind [s3] |
| 3 | Gown | Unfasten ties [s1]<br>Pull the gown away from the neck and shoulders, touching only the inside [s1]<br>Turn the gown inside out as it is removed [s1]<br>Fold or roll the gown into a bundle [s1]<br>Discard the gown in waste or linen receptacle [s1] | Gown front and sleeves are contaminated [s1]<br>Touching only the inside surface keeps contamination away from skin and clothing [s3] | Touching the outside front of the gown with bare hands [s1]<br>Letting the gown brush against scrubs while removing [s3] |
| 4 | Mask or respirator | Grasp the bottom ties or elastic first [s1]<br>Then grasp the top ties or elastic [s1]<br>Lift the mask or respirator forward off the face [s1]<br>Discard in a waste container [s1] | Front of mask or respirator is contaminated and must not be touched [s1]<br>The mask is removed last because removal requires bringing hands close to the face, and at this point all other contaminated PPE has already been discarded [s3] | Touching the front of the mask while removing [s1]<br>Pulling the mask down to the neck and reusing it [needs source] |
| 5 | Hand hygiene | Perform hand hygiene immediately after removing all PPE [s1]<br>Use soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub [s4] | Dirty gloves can soil hands during removal [s4]<br>Hand hygiene after PPE removal closes the contamination chain before the nurse touches anything else [s4] | Skipping hand hygiene because PPE was worn [s4]<br>Delaying hand hygiene until after charting or other tasks [needs source] |

## Notes

- **Cell convention:** each `<br>`-separated item is one atomic Fact. No parentheticals in cells.
- **Two tables, not one.** Don and Doff are presented as two separate Ordered tables in this single Concept file. The structural decision matches `fire-response-race-pass.md` (RACE/PASS) — two distinct canonical sequences kept intact, with the inversion between them being the testable insight. A combined table with a `phase` column was considered and rejected because it would split each canonical sequence across non-adjacent rows and obscure the inversion.
- **The inversion is the point.** Don order: hand hygiene → gown → mask → goggles → gloves. Doff order: gloves → goggles → gown → mask → hand hygiene. The first item donned (hand hygiene) is the last item performed during doffing, and the last item donned (gloves) is the first item removed during doffing. NCLEX-RN tests this reversal directly.
- **Hand hygiene placement at step 1 of donning.** The CDC PPE sequence poster `[s1]` does not list hand hygiene as a numbered donning step on the poster itself, but CDC clinical hand-hygiene guidance `[s4]` and CDC Marburg PPE guidance `[s3]` both explicitly direct hand hygiene before donning gloves or PPE. The NCLEX-RN treats hand hygiene as the first step of the donning sequence, consistent with WHO Five Moments timing. Including it as Row 1 of the Don table reflects the exam's framing without contradicting any source.
- **Hand hygiene placement at end of doffing.** The CDC poster `[s1]` ends with the explicit instruction "PERFORM HAND HYGIENE IMMEDIATELY AFTER REMOVING ALL PPE." This is the unambiguous final step of the doff sequence.
- **Question-form synthesis (Action cells).** Action cells restate the source's imperative steps with mechanical wording transformation (e.g., source: "Fully cover torso from neck to knees" → Fact: "Cover torso from neck to knees"). These are sourced cells with mechanical synthesis, not pedagogical inference.
- **Rationale-cell synthesis.** Several Rationale cells phrase the contamination logic the CDC poster `[s1]` and CDC Marburg guidance `[s3]` describe but do not state in a single sentence. Where the rationale comes from explicit source language, citation is direct (e.g., "Outside of gloves is contaminated" `[s1]`). Where the rationale is the standard pedagogical framing (e.g., "Mask sits beneath the goggle frame to reduce eyewear fogging") and no source phrases it that way, the cell is marked `[needs source]` and listed in the unsourced-values table below.
- **PPE selection vs sequence.** This Concept covers the order of donning and doffing only — it does not cover which PPE items are required for which precaution type. Sibling `isolation-precaution-types.md` (T2 §2.9) covers PPE selection by precaution category. Sibling `transmission-based-precaution-pathogens.md` covers which precaution applies to which pathogen.
- **Fit-check vs seal-check.** Respirator fit-check is the user-performed check at every donning to confirm the seal. Annual fit-testing is a separate OSHA-mandated activity that is out of scope here. The Fact "Fit-check respirator" refers to the per-donning check.
- **Where to don and doff.** The CDC poster `[s1]` directs that PPE be doffed at the doorway or anteroom, except the respirator, which is removed after leaving the room and closing the door. This is location guidance, not sequence guidance, and lives in this note rather than as a Row.
- **Inner vs outer gloves.** Some high-consequence-pathogen protocols (Ebola, Marburg) use double-gloving with the inner glove under the gown cuff and the outer glove over the gown cuff `[s3]`. Standard isolation precautions use single gloves over the gown cuff. The Don sequence above reflects the standard single-glove case, which is what NCLEX-RN tests.

### Tricky distractors

- **First step of donning: hand hygiene vs gown.** A common wrong-answer pattern is selecting "gown" as the first donning step because the CDC poster numbers gown as item 1. Hand hygiene precedes gown per CDC clean-hands guidance `[s4]`; the poster numbers cover the PPE items only.
- **First item to remove: gloves vs gown.** Some learners reverse this because gloves "feel" most contaminated and tempt removal-by-stripping the gown over the gloved hands. The correct sequence is gloves first as discrete items, then gown — never as a combined gown-with-gloves removal in the standard sequence. (CDC publishes a separate combined gown+gloves protocol for laboratory contexts; the NCLEX-tested standard sequence is sequential.)
- **Last item to remove: mask vs hand hygiene.** Mask is the last PPE item removed; hand hygiene is the action that follows. Wrong-answer pattern: choosing hand hygiene as "step before mask removal" because the hands are gloved-then-bare. Hand hygiene comes after the mask is off.
- **Why gloves first, why mask last.** Gloves are the most contaminated surface from patient contact; removing them first prevents transferring contamination to other items still being worn. Mask is removed last because the action requires hands near the face, and by then all other contaminated PPE has already been discarded. Wrong-answer pattern: justifying the order with body-zone reasoning ("upper body last") instead of contamination logic.
- **Touching the front of any item.** The front of every doffed item is contaminated. Goggles are handled by the headband, gown by the inside surface, and mask by the ties or elastic only. Wrong-answer pattern: any technique cell that describes grasping the front surface.
